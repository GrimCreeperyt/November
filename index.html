The Prophecy of Delphi

The Oracle of Delphi was a figure many beleived to be someone who could deliver prophecies from Apollo. Diogenes, after being expelled from Sinope, went to the Oracle to ask what he should do next. The Oracle told him to "Deface the currency". Due to what happened in Sinope he assuemd she meant to deface the political currency, and so he went to Athens to challenge established norms and values.

Diogenes and Slavery

Diogenes was captured by pirates on his way to Aegina and sold as a slave to Xeniades in Corinth.. When asked what his trade was, he said “Govern men.” And so he bade the crier “give notice that if any one wants to purchase a master, there is one here for him.” (227). Diogenes eneded up appointed as the tutor of the sons of Xeniades.

The Death of Diogenes

There are many varied stories regarding the death of Diogenes. Some claim he died of food poisoning from trying to eat raw octopus. Another claims he died of an infection from a dog bite. One even claims that he simply became tired of living and held his breath until he died! there are also variants on how he wanted to be buried. One source says he wanted to be buried on his face as the world would soon be turned upside down. It is beleived this was said due to the rapid and unexpected power shifts happening. Another claims that he wanted to be thrown outside the city to be eaten. When asked if this bothered him, he asked to be given a stick to ward off the animals. When asked how he would ward them off while unconcious, he said if he was unconsious why should he care?
